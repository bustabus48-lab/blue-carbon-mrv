REQUEST FOR PROPOSALS (RFP)
Development of the National Blue Carbon MRV Digital Platform
Republic of Ghana

1. INTRODUCTION
The Government of Ghana, through the Ministry of Environment, Science and Technology (MEST) and the Ministry of Lands and Natural Resources (MLNR), invites qualified firms to submit proposals for the design, development, deployment, and support of a National Blue Carbon Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification (MRV) Digital Platform.
The system will operationalize Ghana’s National Blue Carbon MRV Framework and support:
	•	National GHG inventory reporting
	•	Article 6 participation
	•	Voluntary carbon market programs
	•	Ecosystem management decision-making
	•	Safeguards monitoring
	•	Near-real-time operational monitoring
The platform will be an internal government tool with role-based access, including controlled access for CREMAs (Community Resource Management Areas).
The system must align with:
	•	IPCC Wetlands Supplement
	•	High-integrity carbon accounting practices
	•	Ghana’s national carbon trading registry (minimal duplication)
	•	National data governance requirements

2. OBJECTIVES
The objectives of this assignment are to:
	•	Develop a secure, scalable web-based MRV platform.
	•	Operationalize the national Blue Carbon MRV architecture.
	•	Enable near-real-time ecosystem monitoring.
	•	Provide audit-ready traceability and compliance tracking.
	•	Integrate with the National Carbon Trading Registry (minimal interface).
	•	Ensure role-based governance and institutional separation.
	•	Support high-integrity carbon accounting (≤10% uncertainty).
	•	Enable annual and operational reporting.

1. BACKGROUND
The Government of Ghana has developed a comprehensive National Blue Carbon Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification (MRV) Framework to support:
	•	National Greenhouse Gas (GHG) accounting under the UNFCCC
	•	Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC) implementation
	•	Article 6 participation
	•	Voluntary carbon market engagement
	•	Ecosystem conservation and restoration
	•	Safeguards and governance monitoring
The MRV framework includes high-integrity requirements such as:
	•	≤10% total uncertainty in carbon estimation
	•	≥90% classification accuracy for spatial monitoring
	•	Soil carbon measurement to minimum 1-meter depth
	•	Leakage and permanence accounting
	•	Safeguards and social integrity monitoring
	•	Traceability and audit-ready documentation
To operationalize this framework, the Government intends to establish a secure, scalable, web-based National Blue Carbon MRV Digital Platform.
The platform will serve as an internal government system with controlled access for CREMAs (Community Resource Management Areas) and authorized institutional users.
The registry functionality will be minimal, as Ghana operates a separate National Carbon Trading Registry.

2. OBJECTIVE OF THE ASSIGNMENT
The objective of this consultancy is to design, develop, deploy, and support a fully functional National Blue Carbon MRV Digital Platform that:
	•	Operationalizes the national MRV framework.
	•	Supports near-real-time ecosystem monitoring.
	•	Enables structured field data collection and validation.
	•	Ensures traceability and compliance tracking.
	•	Interfaces with the national carbon registry.
	•	Supports audit and verification processes.
	•	Ensures high levels of data integrity, governance, and cybersecurity.
